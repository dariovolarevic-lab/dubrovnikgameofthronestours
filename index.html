Filming Locations

GoT Filming Locations in Dubrovnik Old Town

Dubrovnik was the primary filming location for King's Landing. Here are some of the legendary spots on our tours.

King's Landing

The Capital of Westeros

Dubrovnik's Old Town became the seat of the Iron Throne from Season 2 onward. The city's medieval walls, terracotta rooftops, and winding limestone streets provided the backdrop for the realm's greatest power struggles — from Joffrey's reign of terror to Daenerys's devastating attack on the capital in Season 8. Nearly every exterior shot of King's Landing was filmed within Dubrovnik's walls.

Pile Gate

City Gates of King's Landing

The western entrance to Dubrovnik's Old Town doubled as the main gate of King's Landing. This is where the infamous riot scene was filmed in Season 2 — King Joffrey is pelted with dung by an angry mob, triggering a violent uprising that Sandor Clegane (The Hound) has to fight through. Your guide walks you through the exact spot where the chaos unfolded.

Old Port

Blackwater Bay

Dubrovnik's old harbour was transformed into Blackwater Bay — the scene of the Battle of the Blackwater in Season 2, where Tyrion used wildfire to destroy Stannis Baratheon's fleet. It's also where Princess Myrcella was sent off to Dorne (Season 2), where Jaime Lannister returned to King's Landing (Season 3), and where Littlefinger's ships were docked. One of the most filmed locations in the entire series.

Fort Lovrijenac

The Red Keep

This 11th-century fortress perched on a 37-metre cliff became the Red Keep — the seat of the Iron Throne and home to the Lannister dynasty. Joffrey's Name Day tournament (Season 2), the Purple Wedding (Season 4), and Tyrion's famous face-slap of Joffrey were all filmed in its courtyard. Fort Lovrijenac's imposing walls are the most recognizable Game of Thrones location in Dubrovnik.

Jesuit Staircase

Cersei's Walk of Shame

The baroque Jesuit Staircase in Dubrovnik's Old Town is where one of the most talked-about scenes in television history was filmed — Cersei Lannister's Walk of Atonement in Season 5. Stripped naked and shorn, Cersei walked down these exact steps while the Septa rang her bell and chanted "Shame!" Lena Headey's body double, Rebecca Van Cleave, walked down 100+ times during the 3-day shoot.

Lokrum Island

Qarth & the Iron Throne

Lokrum Island, a short ferry ride from Old Town, was transformed into the exotic city of Qarth in Season 2. The Benedictine monastery served as the backdrop for Daenerys's meetings with the Thirteen. Today, the monastery houses a Game of Thrones exhibition and one of the few official Iron Throne replicas in the world — a gift from HBO to the city of Dubrovnik. Included in our 3-hour Lokrum Island tour.
